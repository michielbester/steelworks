Key Risks
!
Production cost ceiling. COGS must stay below R10/can to maintain the 40% minimum margin. Any supplier price increase directly compresses profitability.
!
Daily delivery complexity. Golf clubs prefer frequent top-ups (multiple POS points, cold stock). Own delivery at scale is expensive — a distribution partner will likely be required from day one.
!
Recipe loyalty risk. Each club currently has its own Steelworks recipe — this could be a moat (unique to each course) or a barrier. Changing an existing club's recipe risks alienating 10-year loyalists and damaging the club's guest experience.
!
Cannibalization, not new demand. Steelworks is a known SKU at golf clubs. Converting from the house-made version to a canned product may not add a net new sale — it replaces an existing drink and shifts margin risk to the club.
